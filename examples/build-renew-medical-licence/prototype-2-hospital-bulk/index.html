You'll need the individual renewal

The hospital-mediated renewal only works for doctors employed by a public hospital or polyclinic.

If you're in private practice, a locum, or split between public and private work, you'll want the individual renewal instead. It takes a bit longer (about 10 minutes) but it covers every case.

Assumptions to verify

This variant trades speed for institutional coverage. Almost every assumption depends on hospital HR data being good enough to base a regulatory decision on.

About the user

  • Verify with users Doctors will trust the hospital's HR record to be correct enough for a regulatory renewal. (Standard 11)
  • Verify with users A "single tap to confirm" feels reassuring, not flippant, given the legal weight. (Standard 4, Standard 11)
  • Verify with users Doctors actually want their hospital paying the renewal fee from a CPD allowance – or do they prefer to pay personally to keep autonomy?
  • Verify with users The "specialist CPD I haven't told the hospital about" addition is discoverable enough.

About the institutions

  • Verify with MDA The Medical Council will accept hospital HR records as a primary data source, not just doctor self-attestation.
  • Verify with MDA QEH HR (and polyclinic HR) hold CPD hours in a form that's regulatorily complete – or only training the hospital itself runs.
  • Verify with MDA Bulk renewal payments from a hospital CPD allowance are permitted under current finance rules.
  • Verify with MIST An integration with QEH HR can be built without each hospital running a bespoke ETL.

About the cohorts

  • Verify with data What fraction of doctors in Barbados are employed by a public institution that could pre-fill? (Discovery indicates ~50–70% but unconfirmed.)
  • Verify with users Private-practice doctors don't feel second-class when sent to the individual flow. (Standard 3)
  • Verify with users Locums and overseas-rotation doctors aren't worse off than today.
  • Known gap Specialist colleges (RCP, RCS, AAFP) and the data flow from them.

About policy

  • Verify with policy The legal "declaration of fitness to practise" can be made via a digital confirmation, not a wet signature.
  • Verify with policy A hospital can act as a fiduciary in submitting a doctor's regulatory renewal – or is this a Medical Council–doctor relationship only?
  • Verify with policy Data sharing between QEH HR, Medical Council, and Trident ID is permitted under the Data Protection Act for this purpose. (Standard 11)
