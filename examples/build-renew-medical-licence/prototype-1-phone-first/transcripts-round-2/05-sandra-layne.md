**SM:** Thank you for the time, Sandra. I want to do two things – walk through the prototype as if you were a doctor, then have you walk us through what happens on your side. Sound good?

**SL:** Fine. Go on.

[Sandra walks through the prototype quickly. Familiar with the screens already from a previous brief.]

**SL:** Fine. Yes. Yes. Continue. Yes. OK. Paid. Done. Five minutes? I'm not as careful as a real renewer would be. But you can see how it would flow.

**SM:** Anything that jumped out?

**SL:** Two things. First – which doctor is this? Because in our register a doctor isn't just *a doctor*. There's full registration, provisional registration, temporary registration – that's overseas doctors on short-term placements – restricted, which is what some semi-retired doctors have where they can only practise in certain settings, and then we have specialty-overseas which is for our doctors abroad temporarily. So five categories. Your form is the same form for everybody. That won't fly. Each category has different evidence requirements, different fees, sometimes different sign-off.

**SM:** Five categories. That's bigger than the prototype assumes.

**SL:** Exactly. And second – when the doctor hits Pay now, your screen says *Renewal complete*. That's not right. Renewal isn't complete when the doctor pays. The doctor has *applied for renewal*. Then I get the application. I check the CPD against the requirement for that doctor's category. I check that there are no outstanding disciplinary matters. I check that the fee paid matches the category fee. If everything's fine, I or someone senior signs it off, and *then* the renewal is complete. That can be same day if everything is clean. It can be two weeks if I have to chase the doctor for missing evidence.

**SM:** So the confirmation page is misleading.

**SL:** Yes. It should say – I don't know – *Application sent. We're reviewing it.* And then maybe a *We'll email you when it's done* line. Doctors hate not knowing where they stand.

**SM:** Let me ask you about the volume. How many renewals do you see in a typical year?

**SL:** About nine hundred to a thousand altogether across all categories. The peak is October-November-December – that's when seventy per cent come in. By the New Year my desk is invisible under paper.

**SM:** And the most common problems?

**SL:** CPD evidence is always the biggest. People send the wrong thing, or not enough hours, or they forget. About six out of ten renewals need at least one back-and-forth on CPD. The next biggest is people sending the wrong fee – the full-registration fee when they're temporary, or the temporary fee when they should be paying full. The form doesn't help them pick the right fee because we have a paper form that just says "renewal fee".

**SM:** So if the digital form pre-filled the right fee for the category, that would help.

**SL:** It would change my life. Yes.

**SM:** What about disciplinary matters?

**SL:** Five or six renewals a year there's a flag. I can't talk about specifics but it's a process and it needs human judgement. The digital service should hand those to me, not try to handle them.

**SM:** And what about doctors who've passed away? Dr Belgrave mentioned that her late husband still gets renewal letters.

**SL:** [pauses] That's awful. We don't have a clean process for that. I think the right thing is for the Council to find out from somewhere – the GIS, hospital HR, family notification – and update the register. Today I sometimes find out from a phone call from a widow. That's not good enough.

**SM:** This is so helpful. Thank you.

**SL:** One more thing. If you're really going to do this, build *me* a screen too. A queue of applications coming in, with the evidence attached, with traffic-light flags for clean / needs-CPD-chase / has-disciplinary-flag. Don't just leave me with the same paper system but with a different inbox.

**Key signals:**

- **"Renewal complete" is wrong** at the confirmation – the doctor has *applied*; the Council reviews; only then is it complete. Significant microcopy change needed
- **Fee varies by category** – pre-filling the right fee would eliminate one of the two most common renewal errors
- **CPD evidence errors are 60% of renewals** – the "What counts" disclosure and the multi-file pattern are useful, but a CPD-requirement-by-category note is also needed
- **Disciplinary cases need human-in-the-loop**, not automated
- **Deceased-doctor process is broken** today; needs a deliberate fix (Council-side, not citizen-facing)
- **Backstage Council interface is missing entirely** – Sandra has no queue, no flags, no review screen. This is its own discovery
- 70% of annual volume in Oct–Dec; peak handling matters for performance testing
