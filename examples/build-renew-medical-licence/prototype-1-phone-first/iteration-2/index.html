Renew your medical licence

Use this service to renew your annual practising licence with the Medical Council of Barbados.

BBD 200
Renewal fee
Full registration. Different fees apply to provisional and temporary registration.

Before you start

You'll need:

  • Your Trident ID number, or your National Registration Number
  • Your CPD record for the last year (you can add more than one file)
  • A debit or credit card to pay the BBD 200 fee

How long it takes

Most doctors finish this in about 10 minutes. You can pause and come back any time.

When you'll get your certificate

We'll send your new certificate to your registered email within 5 working days. You can print it from the confirmation page too.

If you can't renew online

Call the Medical Council on [FAKE NUMBER] 246-555-0100 and someone will help you. We can also book an appointment at the Council for you.

What's your Trident ID?

This is the 12-digit number on your Trident ID card, or your National Registration Number (NRN).

We'll look up your name, contact details, and current registration. You won't have to type them in.

Pause any time. We'll save your progress and email you a link to come back. Your link is good for 30 days.

Add your CPD records

Add evidence of the Continuing Professional Development you completed this year. You need at least 40 hours.

What counts as CPD?

The Medical Council accepts CPD from:

  • Hospital mandatory training (resuscitation, infection control, safeguarding)
  • Specialty colleges – RCP, RCS, RCOG, Royal College of GPs, AAFP and so on
  • UWI Faculty of Medical Sciences short courses
  • Conferences and Grand Rounds with attendance evidence
  • Online accredited learning (BMJ Learning, NEJM Knowledge+, and similar)
  • Peer-reviewed publications (counted as CPD hours per Medical Council guidance)

Assumptions to verify – round 2

Updated after round-1 user testing with 6 doctors. Resolved assumptions are listed at the bottom; new ones are highlighted.

About the user

  • Verify with users "Pause any time" with a 30-day saved-link genuinely reduces abandonment for time-poor doctors. (sharpened in round 1: needs primary visibility, not buried)
  • Verify with users · NEW Surfacing the BBD 200 fee on the Start page reduces fee surprise without causing pre-flow drop-off.
  • Verify with users · NEW The "Something not right?" alt-options block is read as an invitation, not an admission of failure.
  • Verify with users · NEW Doctors with multiple CPD sources (specialists, hospital staff) find the multi-file pattern natural.
  • Verify with users · NEW Print option is genuinely used and not just requested; data check needed.

About the data

  • Verify with MDA The Council can accept multiple CPD records per renewal, with a combined hours total. (changed in round 1)
  • Verify with MDA Renewal fee differs by registration category (full, provisional, temporary). Each fee correct. (surfaced in round 1)
  • Verify with MDA The "What counts as CPD?" examples (mandatory training, RCP/RCS, UWI, BMJ Learning, peer-reviewed publications) are all accepted by the Council. If any aren't, the disclosure misleads.
  • Verify with data Five working days is a realistic SLA for the certificate. Today's mean is still unknown.
  • Verify with data · NEW Fraction of doctors who use the "Print this page" option on the confirmation page.

About the platform

  • Verify with MIST · SHARPENED Trident ID session state needs to last 30 days for the "Save and come back" promise. (Round 1: MIST flagged default is 30 minutes.)
  • Verify with MIST Trident ID returns medical-licence registration data, or this needs a separate Medical Council register call.
  • Verify with MIST The shared payment gateway supports BBD 200 single transactions with a success webhook.
  • Known gap Multi-file CPD accepts a mix of formats (PDF + photo + text email) – but evidence handoff to the Council's review process needs alignment with how reviewers currently work.

About policy

  • Verify with policy Personal data flow between Trident ID, Medical Council, and the payment gateway is permitted under the Data Protection Act for this purpose. (Standard 11)
  • Verify with policy Doctors can legally complete the renewal entirely digitally – no wet signature required. (Medical Profession Act review needed.)

Resolved in round 1

  • Resolved Doctors will trust a Trident ID lookup for licensing. Confirmed by 5 of 6 in round-1 testing. The sixth was an older doctor who said *"if it's the official site, the government can look me up"* – sceptical but not blocking.
  • Resolved Plain-language labels feel respectful, not patronising. Direct positive comments from 4 of 6, no negative reaction from the other two.
  • Resolved The alpha.gov.bb chrome reads as trustworthy without explanation. All 6 recognised it instantly.
