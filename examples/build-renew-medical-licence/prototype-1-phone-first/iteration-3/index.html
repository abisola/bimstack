Renew your medical licence

Use this service to renew your annual practising licence with the Medical Council of Barbados.

BBD 200
Renewal fee (full registration)
Provisional, temporary, restricted and specialty-overseas registrations have different fees. We'll show you the right one once we know your category.

Before you start

You'll need:

  • Your Trident ID number, or your National Registration Number
  • Your CPD record for the last year (you can add more than one file)
  • A debit or credit card for the renewal fee

How long it takes

Most doctors finish in about 10 minutes. You can pause and come back any time.

You can do this from anywhere in the world

If you're on rotation, on holiday, or working overseas, the service works the same way.

When you'll get your certificate

We'll email your new certificate once the Medical Council has reviewed your application. Most renewals are reviewed within 5 working days.

If you can't renew online

Call the Medical Council on [FAKE NUMBER] 246-555-0100 or book an appointment.

Where will you be practising this year? Pick the one that's most true for you right now. We'll adjust the rest of the renewal to match.
Standard renewal flow

You'll go through the standard renewal: identity, practice details, CPD, payment, confirmation.

What's different if you're abroad

We'll let you give us a different email or postal address while you're away. CPD from your overseas rotation counts – the next step explains what we accept.

You may need a different category

The Medical Council has a restricted registration category for retired or occasional doctors. The CPD requirement and fee are different. Call the Council on 246-555-0100 before you continue if you're not sure. You can still complete the form below and we'll route it to the right reviewer.

Pause any time. We'll save your progress and email you a link to come back.

We'll look up your name, contact details, and current registration category. You won't have to type them in.

Add your CPD records

Add evidence of the Continuing Professional Development you completed this year. For full registration you need at least 40 hours.

Specialists – check your College too

If you hold a specialty qualification (MRCPCH, MRCP, MRCS, FAAP, FACS, FRCS, FRCOG and similar), your College usually requires more hours than the Council's minimum, and may require specialty-specific evidence. Filing 40 Council hours doesn't automatically meet your College's requirement.

What counts as CPD?

The Medical Council accepts CPD from:

  • Hospital mandatory training (resuscitation, infection control, safeguarding)
  • Specialty colleges – RCP, RCS, RCOG, Royal College of GPs, AAFP, AAP, ACOG and similar (UK, US, and Caribbean)
  • Overseas rotations, fellowships, and conferences – activity completed outside Barbados counts, including AAP / ACOG / European Society conferences
  • UWI Faculty of Medical Sciences short courses
  • Online accredited learning (BMJ Learning, NEJM Knowledge+, UpToDate-CME and similar)
  • Peer-reviewed publications (counted per Medical Council guidance)
