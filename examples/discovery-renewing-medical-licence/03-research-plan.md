# User research plan – Renewing a medical licence

**Phase:** Discovery
**Owner:** Service designer
**Date:** 2026-05-16
**Status:** Strawman for week 0. Refine with the Medical Council and BAMP before fieldwork starts.

## Research question

**How do medical practitioners in Barbados currently renew their licence, and what stops it being a simple, reliable, fifteen-minute job done from a phone?**

## Sub-questions

1. What does the current renewal journey look like, step by step, including the paper, the phone calls, the in-person visits, the chasing?
2. Where does the renewal go wrong, and how often – for which categories of doctor?
3. What does the Medical Council's side of the process feel like, and where does staff time go?
4. How does CPD evidence flow today, and what does "good" CPD evidence look like to the Council?
5. What would doctors trust a digital renewal to do, and what would they not trust it with?
6. How does the Ministry of Health and Wellness currently use the register, and what would real-time access change?

## Method mix

| Method | Why | How many | Where | Who runs it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Semi-structured interviews with doctors | Primary signal on the lived experience of renewal | 12–15 across categories | A mix: in-clinic, at the doctor's home, over video for those off-island | Service designer + content & interaction designer |
| Front-line shadow at the Medical Council | Watch real renewals being processed; see the queue, the paper, the chasing | 2 half-days – one mid-cycle, one near a peak | Medical Council premises | Service designer |
| Interviews with Medical Council staff | Hear the process from the operator side | 4–6 staff including the renewals officers, the CPD lead, the Registrar | Medical Council premises | Service designer + delivery manager |
| Interview with BAMP representative | Industry view, representation, sticking points | 1–2 | BAMP office or video | Delivery manager + service designer |
| Interview with Ministry contact | Understand the register's use downstream | 1 | MoHW office | Delivery manager |
| Interview with MIST contact | Understand the platform context (Trident ID feasibility, payment, hosting) | 1 | MIST office | Developer (joins specifically for this) |
| Existing-data review | Volumes, processing time, error rates, support tickets if any | n/a | Documents and reports from the Medical Council | Delivery manager + developer |

Total target: 18–22 conversations across roughly six weeks of fieldwork.

## Recruitment

| Cohort | How we'll recruit | What we'll pay | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Doctors – full registration (public service) | Via hospital HR, BAMP outreach | Modest honorarium, paid to the doctor or to a charity per their preference | Standard 1 – evidence-led, not opportunistic |
| Doctors – full registration (private practice) | Direct BAMP introductions, snowball sampling | Same | Harder to reach – book early |
| Doctors – provisional / probationary | Via the Medical Council's records, with consent | Same | Highest-risk cohort, prioritise |
| Doctors – locums / overseas | Via hospital rotation coordinators, BAMP | Same; videoconference where needed | Likely to surface the assisted-digital edge cases |
| Specialists | Direct introductions via the specialist colleges | Same | At least 2–3 across different specialties |
| Medical Council staff | Direct, with Registrar's support | Their working time – not paid extra | |
| BAMP, MoHW, MIST | Direct via delivery manager | Their working time | |

> Standard 3 (everyone can use it) – we will specifically include at least one doctor working without smartphone access, at least one over 60, and at least one based outside Bridgetown. If we cannot find these doctors, that itself is a finding.

## Outputs

By end of discovery we will have:

- 18–22 anonymised interview transcripts or notes
- Two shadow write-ups from the Medical Council
- Existing-data analysis: volumes, processing time, error rates, peak shape
- A themed user-needs list (typically 5–10 prioritised needs)
- A current-state journey map for each major doctor category
- An ecosystem map showing the Medical Council, Ministry, MIST, BAMP, hospitals, and CPD bodies
- The discovery report and recommendation

## Ethics and consent

- **Plain-language consent** – read aloud, also given on paper. Every participant knows what we'll record, what we'll do with it, what they can refuse, and how to withdraw later. Standard 4 + Standard 11.
- **Recording** – audio only by default; never video. Recordings stored encrypted on GovTech Barbados infrastructure. `[TO VERIFY: align with the Data Protection Act and any Medical Council guidance.]`
- **Anonymisation** – we publish themes and anonymised quotes, never names or anything that could identify a doctor. Specialty, hospital, or unusual circumstance may be redacted to prevent re-identification in a small cohort.
- **Retention** – recordings held for the duration of the discovery plus 90 days for synthesis. Notes retained per the GovTech Barbados research-retention policy. `[TO VERIFY policy exists; if not, propose one.]`
- **Right to withdraw** – participants can withdraw any time before publication; their data is destroyed if they ask.
- **Sensitive material** – if a doctor surfaces a concern about a regulatory matter, professional conduct, or patient safety, we stop recording and route it via the Registrar. We are not investigating.

## What this plan deliberately does not cover

- No quantitative survey in week 1. A survey before we know the questions tends to ask the wrong questions; revisit at week 6 once themes are clear.
- No usability testing yet – nothing has been built. That belongs in alpha.
- No comparative review of overseas digital licensing services. Worth doing in alpha as inspiration, not in discovery as a substitute for talking to Bajan doctors.

## Standards relevant to this plan

- **Standard 1** – Meet user needs. Eighteen-plus conversations is well above the five-minimum floor.
- **Standard 2** – Multidisciplinary team. The plan brings service design, content, delivery, and (briefly) development into the room.
- **Standard 3** – Everyone can use it. The recruitment plan explicitly seeks under-represented voices.
- **Standard 11** – Trust, safety, confidentiality. The ethics section is the discovery's compliance posture.
