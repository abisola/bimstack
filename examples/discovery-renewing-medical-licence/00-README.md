# Discovery kit – Renewing a medical licence

**Strawman, not a finished discovery.** This kit was scaffolded by the bimstack `service-designer` agent against the `discovery-kit` skill, before any conversation with the Medical Council of Barbados or the Ministry of Health and Wellness. It is a starting point for a real discovery, not a substitute for one.

Every uncertain claim is marked `[TO VERIFY]`. The first job of the discovery team is to talk to people and turn those into facts (or strike them out).

## Files in this kit

| File | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `00-README.md` | This file – orientation |
| `01-problem-statement.md` | One-page framing of the problem, in citizen language |
| `02-stakeholder-map.md` | Who's involved, who needs engaging, who decides |
| `03-research-plan.md` | Who we'll talk to, how, when, with what ethics |
| `04-interview-guide.md` | The actual questions for citizen and staff interviews |
| `05-discovery-report-template.md` | Skeleton the team fills in as the discovery runs |

## Standards this kit is anchored to

- **Standard 1** – Make sure your service meets your users' needs
- **Standard 2** – Discover, design, build and deliver with a multidisciplinary team
- **Standard 4** – Use simple and relatable language
- **Standard 7** – Use open, common, interoperable platforms
- **Standard 11** – Design for trust, safety, and confidentiality

## How to use it

1. Read this README, then the problem statement.
2. With the delivery manager, refine the stakeholder map and book the first round of conversations.
3. Use the research plan and interview guide for the first 2–3 weeks of discovery.
4. Fill in the discovery report template as findings emerge.
5. End the discovery with a clear recommendation: proceed to alpha, redesign the problem, or stop.

Working assumption for this strawman: **8–12 week discovery**, **team of 4** (service designer, content & interaction designer, delivery manager, subject-matter expert from the Medical Council), with the developer and cyber engineer joining at alpha. [TO VERIFY with the MDA.]
