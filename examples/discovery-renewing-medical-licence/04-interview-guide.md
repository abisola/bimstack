# Interview guide – Doctors renewing a medical licence

**For:** Semi-structured interviews with practising medical doctors in Barbados
**Duration:** 45–60 minutes
**Two versions:** Section A for doctors; Section B for Medical Council staff (further down)

---

## Section A – Doctors

### Before we start (5 minutes)

- Thanks for the time. Brief intro of who we are (GovTech Barbados, bimstack discovery team) and why we're here.
- We're trying to understand how you renew your licence today – what works, what doesn't, what you wish were different.
- This is research, not policy. We won't change anything based on this conversation alone, and you won't be quoted by name.
- Consent:
  - Can we record audio? (Stop and explain what happens to the recording: kept encrypted, transcribed, deleted after 90 days, never shared outside the team. You can ask us to stop or delete it any time.)
  - Can we use anonymised quotes in our report? (Yes/no, fine either way.)
  - You can stop the interview at any moment and you don't need a reason.

### Warm-up (5–10 minutes)

We're not after credentials – just enough to understand your situation.

- Tell me about a typical working week.
- Where do you practise? Hospital, polyclinic, private clinic, mixed?
- How long have you been registered to practise in Barbados?
- Do you work elsewhere as well – another island, a UK or US locum, anything like that?

### Core – the renewal experience (25–30 minutes)

Walk me through your most recent renewal.

- When did you start thinking about renewing this time? What triggered it?
- Walk me through what you did, step by step. (Listen for: where they got the form, what they had to gather, who they called, how long the paperwork took, whether anything went wrong, whether they had to make a second trip.)
- What was the easiest part?
- What was the hardest part?
- How long did the whole thing take – from "I should probably do this" to "done"?
- How much of that was clinical time you lost?
- What did you do that worked around the process? (Listen for: getting an admin person to do it, doing it during a clinic break, calling someone they know at the Council, etc.)

CPD evidence:

- How do you keep track of your CPD through the year?
- When the renewal asks for evidence, what do you actually submit, and in what form?
- Has it ever bounced back? What happened?
- If you do specialist CPD as well, how does that fit in?

Money:

- How do you pay the fee? Where? At what point?
- Has a payment ever gone wrong, or got separated from the application?

When it goes wrong:

- Have you, or anyone you know, had a renewal lapse or come close? What happened?
- How did you find out something was missing?
- How did you find out it was fixed?

### Trust and digital (5–10 minutes)

- If you could renew this entirely from your phone, would you?
- What would you trust it to handle? What would you not trust it with?
- Some services in Barbados use Trident ID for personal details – would that be the right starting point here, or does a doctor need something else?
- Where would you be doing the renewal if it was on a phone – at work, at home, between patients?
- What's your phone, your data plan, your typical connection like at the hospital or clinic?

### Close (3–5 minutes)

- Is there anything we should have asked but didn't?
- Who else should we talk to – a colleague in a different category, someone newly registered, someone who's had a tricky renewal?
- Any questions for us?
- Thanks. Honorarium / next steps / how to reach us if anything comes up.

## Section B – Medical Council staff

Adapt the guide above. The shape stays the same; the focus shifts.

### Warm-up

- What's your role at the Medical Council?
- How long have you been doing it?
- What does a typical week look like?

### Core – the renewal process from the inside

- Walk me through what happens when a doctor's renewal comes in.
- Where does most of your time go in a renewal? Where does most of the friction sit?
- What's the queue like? Is there a peak time of year? What happens during the peak?
- What's most often wrong with what doctors send in?
- How do you chase doctors when something's missing?
- How do you tell a doctor their renewal is done?
- Is there a moment in the process where you wish a system did something for you?

CPD evidence:

- What does good CPD evidence look like to you? What's not good enough?
- How do you store the evidence after you've checked it?

The register:

- How is the register updated when a renewal completes?
- Who outside the Council relies on the register being current?
- What happens when someone calls to ask if a doctor is currently registered?

When it goes wrong:

- Tell me about a renewal that went wrong recently. What happened? How was it resolved?
- Has a renewal lapse ever caused a practical problem in a hospital or clinic that you heard about?

### Future

- If you could redesign one part of the renewal process, what would it be?
- What would you not want changed?
- What would worry you about doing this digitally?
- Who else in the Council should we talk to?

### Close

- What have we not asked that we should have?
- Thanks. Next steps.

## Probes the interviewer keeps in their back pocket

Use sparingly when an answer is general. They get to the specific story underneath.

- "Tell me about the last time that happened."
- "Walk me through that, step by step."
- "What did you do next?"
- "Who did you call?"
- "What did you actually see on the screen / on the form / in the office?"
- "How long did that take?"
- "What were you thinking at that moment?"
- "What did you do instead?"
- "Show me, if you can – do you have it on your phone?"

## Standards relevant to this interview guide

- **Standard 1** – Meet user needs. The guide is built around lived experience, not assumptions.
- **Standard 4** – Simple, relatable language. The guide is written the way the interviewer would actually speak. No jargon for the participants.
- **Standard 11** – Trust, safety, confidentiality. The consent process is plain, explicit, and revocable.

## What good notes look like

For each interview, the researcher captures: date, anonymous participant ID, category (e.g. "private-practice GP, 15+ years registered, Bridgetown"), 5–10 verbatim quotes, the journey as the participant described it, and 3–5 surprises. Themes emerge in synthesis, not in the interview itself.
