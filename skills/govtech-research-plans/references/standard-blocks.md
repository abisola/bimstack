# Standard blocks

These blocks are baked into every plan. Scripts are adapted to the project (fill the {braces}, swap the service description) but keep their structure and key lines.

## Intro script (Part 2, [INTRO])

> *Hi, I'm {Facilitator's name}, a researcher working with GovTech, a state-owned enterprise working to improve the ways people interact with local public services. We're working on {one-sentence plain description of the service/topic}. I'm here with my colleague {Note-taker's name} and we'd love your feedback.*
>
> *Just to be clear — we're testing the website, not you. There are no wrong answers, and if anything is confusing, that's a problem with our design, not with you.*

For discovery interviews (no thing to test), the second paragraph becomes: *"There are no wrong answers — we're here to learn from your experience, not to check anything about you."*

## Consent script (Part 2, [CONSENT])

> *Before we start, is it OK if we record audio and take a few notes? Anything you share will be totally anonymous — we won't use your name. You can stop at any time.*

When the session involves form tasks, append: *"And one important thing: everything you type into the form today will be made-up details we give you — you'll never enter your real ID number or address. Is that OK?"*

When the topic touches the project's sensitivity directly (health, money, family), append a line modelled on: *"You never have to tell us anything about your own {health/finances/situation}."*

Always followed by the facilitator note: *Wait for a clear yes before starting the recorder. If no to audio, continue with notes only and mark the session sheet 'notes only'.*

## Background questions (adapt the middle, keep the frame)

Open with: *"I'd like to start with a couple of general questions first, just to make sure we're getting a broad range of feedback."*

Standard items: approximate age (doesn't need to be exact) · online services used regularly · main device · self-rated tech comfort (very / somewhat / not very) · 2–4 project-specific experience questions (these decide flow assignment where flows are split — say so in a facilitator note) · consent to hold contact details for follow-up (recorded separately from notes).

## Observation prompts (usability sessions)

While observing, only use gentle prompts like: What are you thinking now? · What were you expecting to see here? · What made you choose that option? · Is there anything confusing on this page? · What would you do next? · At the end of every page, before they move on, ask what they would expect to happen next.

Close the section with the bolded line: **No hinting. No guiding. Just gentle prompts if they go totally silent.**

## Observation codes (Part 3)

| Code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| S | Success — completed step without help |
| H | Hesitation — paused, re-read, backtracked |
| E | Error — wrong path, wrong answer, misread |
| A | Assist — facilitator had to intervene (task fails 'unassisted') |
| Q | Verbatim quote — capture word for word, in dialect as spoken |
| B | Bug/technical — prototype broke, upload failed, keyboard/input issue |
| I | Idea/expectation — participant expected a feature that isn't there |
| + | Delight — something that clearly worked well or pleased them |

For discovery interviews drop S/E/A (there are no tasks to succeed at); keep H (hesitation still signals sensitive or confusing territory), Q, B, I, + and add topic/theme codes named for the discussion topics (T1, T2, …). For concept testing keep the full set but redefine E as "misunderstood the concept" and A as "needed the concept explained".

## Severity scale (Part 3, applied at team debrief, not live)

| Severity | Definition |
|---|---|
| Critical | User would take a wrong real-world action or abandon the service |
| Major | Significant struggle or misunderstanding; recovers only with difficulty |
| Minor | Friction or hesitation; self-recovers |
| Observation | Worth noting; no user harm |

The definitions are constant. The **examples column is written fresh per project** from the worst real-world wrong actions this specific service could cause (see SKILL.md Step 2, rule 5).

## Ethics section — constant lines

Every plan's ethics section contains, alongside the project-specific sensitivity paragraph:

- Verbal consent for audio recording and notes, captured at the start of each session (script in the discussion guide).
- All notes anonymised at the point of capture (P1, P2, …). Contact details for follow-up stored separately from session notes and only with explicit consent.
- Participants can stop, skip a question, or withdraw at any time with no effect on their access to any service.
- Comply with the Barbados Data Protection Act 2019: collect the minimum, state the purpose, delete recordings after analysis (target: 30 days).
- Where forms are completed: all task data is fictional; participants never enter real personal details.

## Recruitment — constant lines

- Screen out anyone who works in digital government or in the department that runs the service being tested.
- Offer a small thank-you (e.g. phone credit or grocery voucher) per GovTech's incentive practice.
- Parish spread: require at least 3 parishes represented, not only St Michael / Christ Church.
- Include a "fresh eyes" quota (1–2 people who've never used the service or been through the existing process).

## Ratings (Part 3, end of session)

Three rows, adapted in wording to the project: Confidence (1–5, phrased around real-world readiness, e.g. "How ready would you feel for your event based on what you did today?") · Ease (1 very hard – 5 very easy) · Likelihood to use for real (Yes / maybe / no + why, versus the current paper/in-person process where one exists).

## Closing script

> *Thanks for your time today. We really appreciate your thoughts and experiences — it will help improve this service for {who it serves} across Barbados. Have a good day!*
