# Method reference — moderated usability testing

Use for: an existing prototype or live service, where the question is whether real people can understand it, complete it, and act correctly on it in the real world.

## Method defaults

- Moderated, task-based, think-aloud. In-person, 1:1. One facilitator + one note-taker; roles can rotate between sessions but never within one.
- Participant's own smartphone via QR code, team device as fallback — alpha.gov.bb services are used on mobile in real life, so mobile realism matters. Note in the method table how upload test files reach the participant (pre-loaded team device; WhatsApp to own phone; or switch device for the upload step only).
- 8–12 participants total. Single audience: 8–10. Split flows: 10–12 with the quota split per flow, and at least 8 completed full sessions as the floor.
- Session length 30–45 minutes per flow. Always define the intercept-compressed version (20–25 minutes, first task only).
- Recording: audio only with consent; written notes are the primary record.
- Recruitment leans on intercepts where the audience naturally is (polyclinics, markets, vending areas — with site permission), plus pre-scheduled sessions for harder quotas (older adults, low tech comfort, specific roles like organisers).

## Task design rules

1. **3–4 tasks per flow, ordered as the real journey**: understand (start page) → do (complete the thing) → edge case or linked service. The first task always tests the start page cold: can they work out whether the service applies to them, the deadline, the cost, and what to have ready?
2. **Scenarios are specific, third-person or fictional-first-person, and Bajan-real** — a named festival, a plausible menu, a parish that is not the participant's own when location matters. Specificity is what makes think-aloud data comparable across participants.
3. **Every scenario embeds the facts needed to judge comprehension.** If the correct answer to "does she qualify?" depends on a two-part rule, the scenario supplies exactly one part obviously and one part subtly.
4. **The data card** (for form tasks): fictional name, NRN in the format the form hints (e.g. 870315-1234), address, phone, email, plus every scenario-specific fact — except the deliberately omitted "don't know" item (SKILL.md Step 2, rule 4). List the card contents in the facilitator note.
5. **Mid-form change task**: at Check your answers, have the facilitator introduce a change ("the organiser calls — the end time is now 9pm, can you fix that?") to test the Change links and return-to-summary behaviour.
6. **Never explain the declaration.** Let them read and tick, then probe understanding afterwards: "In your own words, what did you just agree to?" Typed-name-as-signature and any cost-responsibility checkbox get their own comprehension checks.
7. **Comprehension checks come after reading, before moving on** — phrased as the real-world question ("So — can your aunt get help? What makes you say that?"), never as a quiz about the page.

## Facilitator notes must cover, per task

Where the participant starts (which page) · why the scenario is built the way it is (what rule or distinction it isolates) · the correct answers · what to watch for specifically · when (if ever) to prompt, and with what wording · device/file logistics for that task.

## Note-taking framework, per task

- "Watch for and record" bullets mirroring the facilitator note's watch-fors — first-10-seconds behaviour, specific label reactions, route taken, key term reactions.
- A step-result table (rows = the steps of that task, columns = Result (S/H/E/A) + Notes/quotes).
- A comprehension table (rows = each check, columns = Correct answer + P's answer (right/partial/wrong) + Notes/quotes). The correct answer is always filled in.

## Research questions

8–11 RQs, numbered, each mapped to a prototype area in the table. Always end with the catch-all: "Where do users hesitate, misread, or lose confidence — and why?" (all pages). When two services are linked, at least one RQ addresses the linkage explicitly (do users discover the second service, and by what route?).
